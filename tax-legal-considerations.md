# Tax & Legal Considerations for South Africans

## Important: SARS Requirements

### Income Declaration
- All international remote work income MUST be declared to SARS
- Register as a provisional taxpayer if income exceeds R30,000/year
- Use income tax return (ITR12) to declare foreign income

### Tax Rates
- South African residents are taxed on worldwide income
- Progressive rates from 18% to 45% depending on income bracket
- Foreign tax credits may apply if double-taxed

### Practical Steps
1. Keep records of ALL payments received
2. Track exchange rates at time of receipt
3. Consider consulting a tax professional for large amounts

---

## Work Visa Considerations

- South African citizens can work remotely for foreign companies without special visa
- No requirement for work permits for remote work
- Must ensure compliance with tax residency rules

---

## Payment Method Tax Implications

- PayPal/Payoneer payments are treated as self-employment income
- Bank transfers are same as any income
- Keep all payment records for tax purposes

---

## Recommended Actions

1. **Create business bank account** - Separates personal and business finances
2. **Use accounting software** - Track income/expenses
3. **Consider SARS eFiling** - Register for easy tax submissions
4. **Keep 25-30% aside** - For tax payments from gross income

---

## Payment Methods for South Africans

Most platforms support:
- **PayPal** - Most common, but watch fees
- **Payoneer** - Recommended for better exchange rates
- **Bank Transfer (Wire)** - Sometimes available but slower
- **Payoneer Mastercard** - Can be used as debit card

**Tip:** Open a Payoneer account as it often offers 10-15% better exchange rates than PayPal.
